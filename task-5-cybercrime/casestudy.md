# Identity Theft Case Study

Identity theft is a type of cybercrime where a person’s personal information is stolen and used without their permission for fraudulent activities. This information may include name, phone number, bank details, Aadhaar number, or login credentials.

In a typical case, the attacker collects personal data through phishing emails, fake websites, or social media. For example, a student may receive a message asking them to update their bank details through a link. When they enter their information, it is captured by the attacker. The attacker then uses this data to access bank accounts, make transactions, or create fake accounts in the victim’s name.

Students and young individuals are often targeted because they may not be fully aware of such scams. The consequences of identity theft include financial loss, damage to reputation, and misuse of personal identity.

This case highlights the importance of protecting personal information and being cautious while sharing details online.
