# Identity Theft Case Study

Identity theft is a serious cybercrime in which an individual’s personal information is stolen and misused without their consent. This information may include details such as name, phone number, Aadhaar number, bank account information, or login credentials. With the increasing use of digital platforms, identity theft has become more common, especially among students and young users.

In a typical case, an attacker collects personal data through phishing emails, fake websites, or social media interactions. For instance, a student may receive an email or message that appears to be from a trusted organization, asking them to update their personal or banking details through a provided link. When the student enters this information, it is captured by the attacker. The attacker can then use these details to access bank accounts, perform unauthorized transactions, or create fake accounts in the victim’s name.

Students are often targeted because they may lack awareness about such scams and may trust online sources easily. The consequences of identity theft can be severe, including financial loss, emotional stress, and damage to personal reputation.

This case highlights the importance of being cautious while sharing personal information online and staying aware of potential cyber threats.
